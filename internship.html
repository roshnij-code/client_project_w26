Planning Your Job/Internship Search

Overview

A successful job or internship search requires planning, organization, and persistence. Whether you're seeking your first internship, a summer position, or a full-time role after graduation, this guide will help you develop a strategic approach to finding opportunities that align with your career goals.

The School of Information prepares students for diverse careers in UX research and design, data science, library and information science, information policy, product management, and more. Your search strategy should reflect the unique aspects of your field while leveraging UMSI's extensive resources and network.

Getting Started

Self-Assessment

Before diving into applications, take time to reflect on your goals and preferences:

  • Career interests: What type of work excites you? (Research, design, analysis, development, etc.)
  • Industry preferences: Healthcare, finance, tech, nonprofit, government, education?
  • Company size and culture: Startup, mid-size, or large corporation? Fast-paced or structured?
  • Location: Where do you want to work? Are you open to remote positions?
  • Skills you want to develop: What do you want to learn in this role?
  • Work-life balance: What are your priorities regarding schedule and flexibility?
  • Compensation needs: What are your financial requirements and expectations?

Define Your Target Roles

Identify 3-5 job titles or role types that align with your interests:

  • UX Researcher, UX Designer, Product Designer
  • Data Analyst, Data Scientist, Business Intelligence Analyst
  • Reference Librarian, Digital Archivist, Metadata Specialist
  • Product Manager, Technical Program Manager
  • Information Analyst, Knowledge Manager, Information Architect

Assess Your Readiness

Ensure you have the foundation for a successful search:

  • Updated resume tailored to your target roles
  • Strong cover letter template you can customize
  • LinkedIn profile that's complete and professional
  • Portfolio website (essential for UX/design roles, recommended for others)
  • List of references who've agreed to recommend you
  • Clear understanding of your skills and how to articulate them

Application Strategy

Quality Over Quantity

Rather than mass-applying to hundreds of positions, focus on quality applications:

  • Target positions that genuinely match your skills and interests
  • Customize your resume and cover letter for each application
  • Research the company before applying
  • Aim for 5-10 quality applications per week rather than 50 generic ones
